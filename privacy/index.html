Privacy Policy

Privacy policy for apps published by Maxim Burlak.

This policy applies to mobile applications published by Maxim Burlak, including Network Tools, unless a specific app links to a different policy.

Last updated: March 31, 2026

Who operates these apps

The apps are operated by Maxim Burlak. For privacy questions, contact msburlak@yandex.ru.

Information processed on your device

The apps are designed to keep most data on your device. Depending on the feature you use, the app may store diagnostic history, saved run snapshots, feature settings, user preferences, and server configuration entered by you.

Network information used by the apps

Some features process network information because that is their core function. This may include IP addresses, domain names, route hops, DNS responses, open ports, or local device responses that you request in the app.

Network Tools can also send test traffic to a speed test server that you configure. When you use that feature, the configured server may receive the request metadata normally visible to a server, including your IP address and timing information.

Data sharing

The apps do not sell personal information and do not use third-party advertising SDKs. Data is only sent to a third party when it is required to perform a feature that you explicitly use, such as a speed test endpoint that you configure yourself.

Analytics and tracking

The apps do not use third-party analytics or cross-app tracking unless a future app version explicitly states otherwise in this policy and in App Store privacy disclosures.

Data retention and deletion

Locally stored diagnostic history and preferences remain on your device until you remove them, clear them in the app, or uninstall the app. If a feature communicates with a server that you control or configure, retention on that server depends on how that server is operated.

Children’s privacy

These apps are not directed to children under 13, and the developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children through the apps.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The latest published version on this page is the version that applies.

If a future app has materially different data practices, this policy must be updated or that app must link to a separate privacy page.
